QS Universirty Ranks 2017-2022 visualisation                                                                  

The visualisation is to show the top performaing universities in the world so that they can be compared when students are deciding where to attend to complete their degree. We will look at the university breakdown globally and then break this down to the top performing universities in the world.

Below we can see a world map showing the countries where the top universities in the world reside, as well as the amount of international students from other countries that study in the particular country per 10,000 population.

Click on the buttons below to see the map shoing the faculty counts for these same universities. Can you see the comparisons from both maps? What countries have the biggest changes from students to faculty?

Below if the breakdown of the student to faculty ratio for each country so we can see the disparity between countries. From this bar chart, we can see that the average rartio is roughly 14, which means there are approximately 14 students for every faculty memeber at univerisites in these countries. However, there are some major outliers with countries like Syria and Morocco having a much larger ratio.

Here we can see the top ranked univeristies in the world. We can see that most of them resisde in the US with 60% of the top 10 universities based in America. We also have the likes of England and Switzerland appearing in the top ranked universities. Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) has stayed at number 1 for the last 5 years which shows its prestige and high calibre student output. This year the University of Oxford moved 3 places from last year to 2nd in the top 10 showing its strong improvement, as well as University of Cambridge which moved from 7th to 3rd, showing that England's university standards have increased phenomenally over the last year.

Here we can see the score distribution the top 10 univerisities. As expected MIT have led the world with a perfect score for the last 5 years. We can also the the massive increase of the University of Oxford scores which led to them jumping to the number 2 spot this year. The biggest discrpency of the scores if from the Univeristy of Cambridge, Imperial College London and University of Oxford. This shows that England's education standards increased substantially in the last 5 yeards comparatively to other countries.

Here we can see the distribution of university sizes, taking all the universities in the world and grouping them into sizes. It is evident that almost half of thw worlds universities are considered large (meaing they contain more than 12,000 students, but less than 30,000). Medium (greater than 5,000 students, but less than 12,000 students) and Extra large (greater than 30,000 students) sized universities are quite similar in distibution. Small universities (Less than 5,000 students) are not very common accross the world meaining it may be hard for these facilities to run with a low student count.
