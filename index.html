QS Universirty Ranks 2017-2022 Visualisation

This visualisation is to show the top performing universities in the world so that they can be compared when students are deciding where to attend to complete their degree.

Distribution of international students and faculty around the world

Below we can see a world map showing the spread of international students and faculty for universities in the associated countries per 10,000 population.

Click on the buttons below to see the difference between student and faculty count. Can you see the comparisons from both maps? What countries have the biggest changes from students to faculty?

Benathe the map is a breakdown of the student to faculty ratios for each country so we can see the disparity between countries. From this bar chart, we can see that the average rartio is roughly 14, which means there are approximately 14 students for every faculty memeber at univerisites in these countries. However, there are some major outliers with countries like Syria and France having a much larger ratio.

Top 10 universities in the world

Here we can see the top ranked univeristies in the world. Most of them resisde in the US with 60% of the top 10 universities based in America. Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) has stayed at number 1 for the last 5 years. England dominate 30% of the top 10 universities and the chart shows that their university standards have increased phenomenally over the last year, with Oxford and Cambridge going up multiple ranks in the last year.

Scores of the top 10 universities in the world

Here we can see the score distribution the top 10 univerisities. MIT have led the world with a perfect score for the last 5 years. England's education standards increased substantially in the last 5 years comparatively to other countries.

Size distribution of universities around the world

Here we can see the distribution of university sizes. Almost half of thw worlds universities are considered large (contain more than 12,000 students, but less than 30,000). Medium (greater than 5,000 students, but less than 12,000 students) and Extra large (greater than 30,000 students) sized universities are similar in distibution. Small universities (Less than 5,000 students) are not very common accross the world.
